## Context

One of my first tasks on Pleo’s design systems team was to redesign our focus indicator. It was a `2px` blue ring, which designers had long complained looked off-brand. It even spawned a Slack emoji of our team lead’s face outlined in blue.

## Research

Given the brief task description, I started by researching the following questions.

### What is a focus indicator?

A focus indicator is a visual cue (usually an outline) highlighting the currently focused element for keyboard users. Without it, sighted keyboard users can’t track their position or interactions, similar to a mouse cursor for mouse users.

### What do designers dislike about the existing focus indicator?

I interviewed five designers and identified three key issues:

<HighlightList>
  <Highlight title="Colour" icon="colour">
    The blue ring felt off-brand, black was preferred.
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight title="Contrast" icon="contrast">
    Poor visibility against dark component borders.
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight title="Spacing" icon="spacing">
    The ring sat too close to elements, feeling cramped.
  </Highlight>
</HighlightList>

### What do other design systems do?

Reviewing the focus indicators of [Adobe](https://spectrum.adobe.com/), [IBM](https://carbondesignsystem.com/), [Shopify](https://polaris-react.shopify.com/?shpxid=7a7803af-B33A-4BF9-6C1D-56FB1D5B9FB2), and [Atlassian](https://atlassian.design/) revealed common patterns:

- `2px` blue focus indicators are the norm
- `box-shadow` or `outline` are preferred over `border` or `background`
- Both inset and offset rings are used, depending on the component

### What are the accessibility requirements?

Based on [WCAG 2.2](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/focus-appearance.html) and [Sara Soueidan’s guide](https://www.sarasoueidan.com/blog/focus-indicators/#new-focus-indicator-accessibility-requirements-in-wcag-2.2), focus indicators must:

<HighlightList>
  <Highlight icon="focusUnfocus">
    Feature at least 3:1 contrast between focused and unfocused states
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight icon="area">
    Cover a minimum area equivalent to a `2px` perimeter
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight icon="contrast">
    Maintain at least 3:1 contrast against adjacent colours
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight icon="visibility">
    Remain unobscured by surrounding content
  </Highlight>
</HighlightList>

### What about Windows High Contrast Mode?

Windows High Contrast Mode is an OS-level feature that applies high-contrast colour schemes across applications. [Over 50% of low-vision users rely on some form of high-contrast mode](https://webaim.org/projects/lowvisionsurvey2/#at), making support essential.

Focus indicators should use a visible `outline` or a transparent fallback, as [Windows High Contrast Mode disables box-shadows](https://accessabilly.com/accessibility-issues-concerning-windows-high-contrast-mode/).

### What’s the best way to style a focus indicator?

I reviewed the trade-offs of [different CSS approaches](https://adhoc.team/2022/02/08/creating-focus-style-for-themable-design-system), all of which had downsides:

<HighlightList>
  <Highlight title="Outline" icon="outline">
    Ignores `border-radius`, limited styling options, no multi-colour support.
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight title="Border" icon="border">
    Affects size and layout, difficult to control spacing from focused element,
    can clash with letter descenders, limited styling options, no multi-colour
    support, limited Windows High Contrast Mode support.
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight title="Text-decoration underline" icon="underline">
    Cannot be consistently applied to all elements, ignores `border-radius`,
    limited styling options, no multi-colour support.
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight title="Background colour" icon="background">
    Limited styling options, no multi-colour support, limited Windows High
    Contrast Mode support.
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight title="Box-shadow" icon="shadow">
    Difficult to control spacing from the focused element, limited Windows High
    Contrast Mode support.
  </Highlight>
</HighlightList>

## Requirements

I created the following requirements based on the task description and research:

<HighlightList>
  <Highlight icon="design">
    Address concerns about contrast, spacing, and brand alignment
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight icon="accessibility">
    Be accessible (including support for Windows High Contrast Mode)
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight icon="shadow">
    Use `box-shadow` (with `outline` fallback)
  </Highlight>
</HighlightList>

## Result

### Variants

I introduced three focus ring variants to support different UI contexts.

  <Image
    src={RegularVariant}
    alt="Borderless components with focus rings sitting flush against the element."
  />
  <figcaption>
    **Regular.** Sits flush against the focused element. Works well for elements
    with padding but no border.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

<figure>
  <Image
    src={OffsetVariant}
    alt="Components with (dark) borders and offset focus rings separated by a white spacer."
  />
  <figcaption>
    **Offset.** Designed for elements with (dark) borders. A white spacer
    increases separation and improves visibility, addressing feedback about
    tight spacing.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

<figure>
  <Image
    src={InsetVariant}
    alt="UI components with inset focus rings drawn inside the element."
  />
  <figcaption>
    **Inset.** Used where space is constrained and external focus rings risk
    being clipped.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

### Colour

I changed the focus ring from blue (`#4588e3`) to pure black (`#000000`), which is part of [Pleo’s core brand palette](https://www.pleobrand.site/colour/). This addressed designer feedback that the previous colour felt off-brand.

Pure black also simplified accessibility compliance. It provides maximum contrast against Pleo’s default white backgrounds and comfortably exceeds the 3:1 requirement even on the darkest supported background.

<figure>
  <Image
    src={Colour}
    alt="Comparison of the old blue focus ring and the new black one shown against the darkest supported gray background."
  />
  <figcaption>
    The blue focus ring only barely exceeded the 3:1 contrast requirement on top
    of the darkest supported background (3.02:1), while the new black focus ring
    had no such problem (17.77:1).
  </figcaption>
</figure>

### Size

I increased the focus ring thickness from `2px` to `3px`. While `2px` was sufficient for the `regular` and `offset` variants, the `inset` variant required a slightly thicker ring to meet the [minimum contrasting area](https://www.sarasoueidan.com/blog/focus-indicators/#2.-minimum-contrasting-area) requirement. To avoid inconsistent sizing across variants, I standardised on `3px`.

### Box-shadow and outline fallback

I used box-shadows to implement the focus rings, as they offer the most flexible styling and allow the focus ring to follow an element’s border radius.

The `offset` variant is created by layering a black box-shadow on top of a white one, visually separating the ring from the element on white backgrounds.

Because [Windows High Contrast Mode hides box-shadows](https://accessabilly.com/accessibility-issues-concerning-windows-high-contrast-mode/), I added transparent outlines as a fallback. These remain invisible by default but become visible when Windows High Contrast Mode is enabled.

<figure>
  <Image
    src={WindowsHighContrastMode}
    alt="Button with focus styling shown in normal mode and in Windows High Contrast Mode."
  />
  <figcaption>
    When High Contrast Mode is enabled (bottom), system colours replace the UI's
    default colours (top). The transparent outline fallback keeps the focus ring
    visible.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

### Semantic tokens

I created semantic tokens exposing key styling parameters:

- `colourShadowFocus`
- `colourShadowFocusInvalid`
- `sizeFocusRing`

This enabled product teams to implement custom focus rings where necessary, while maintaining alignment with the design system’s accessibility and visual standards.

  <Image
    src={SemanticToken}

  />
  <figcaption>
    The semantic tokens were also used to define focus rings for form elements
    in invalid states, ensuring consistent styling across validation states.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

## Impact

The redesigned focus indicator was standardised in the design system and adopted across Pleo’s product teams. Designers reported that it now matched the brand, and feedback about cramped spacing and insufficient contrast against component borders was eliminated.

The implementation met WCAG 2.2 requirements and improved support for Windows High Contrast Mode. Semantic tokens reduced ad-hoc implementations, giving teams a consistent way to customise focus indicators while staying aligned with the system.

Overall, the work reduced design debt, improved cross-team consistency, and increased confidence that keyboard focus was accessible and visually intentional.

## Reflections

If I were to redo this project today, here are some things I'd do differently:

<HighlightList>
  <Highlight title="Use outline instead of box-shadow" icon="outline">
    All modern browsers now support `border-radius` for outlines, removing the
    need to use box-shadows and simplifying implementation.
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight title="Replace polyfill with focus-visible" icon="focus">
    All modern browsers support it now, plus it makes focus debugging
    significantly clearer.
  </Highlight>
  <Highlight title="Revisit animation curve" icon="animation">
    A faster ease-out or custom timing function would better reflect the purpose
    of a focus indicator, which should appear almost immediately.
  </Highlight>
</HighlightList>
